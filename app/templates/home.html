About LecFlow

What is LecFlow?

A lecture management platform designed specifically for the IIT Madras BS program.

🎯

Purpose

LecFlow is built to solve a specific problem: helping IIT Madras BS students manage their lecture schedules, recordings, and notes without juggling multiple tools like Google Calendar, Drive, Notion, and WhatsApp.

⚡

How It Works

Simply sign in with your Google account, paste your IIT Madras iCal URL, and LecFlow automatically fetches all your lectures. Each session gets its own page with the recording link and space for notes.

👥

Who It's For

Any student in the IIT Madras BS program — whether you're in Foundation, Diploma, or Degree level. If you have an iCal feed and Google Drive recordings, LecFlow works for you.

Features

Everything in One Place

Designed around the actual IIT Madras BS workflow — not a generic student planner with the logo swapped out.

📅

iCal Calendar Sync

Pulls your live sessions straight from the IIT Madras iCal feed, including recurring events and rescheduled classes — no manual entry needed.

🎥

Lecture Recordings

Watch Google Drive recordings inline without leaving the app. Each event links directly to the right file — no hunting through shared folders.

📝

Per-Lecture Notes

Write notes tied to a specific session. They autosave as you type and are ready the next time you revisit that lecture.

🔁

Recurring Event Handling

The app correctly expands RRULE recurrences, so weekly live sessions show up as individual, trackable events — not a single ambiguous entry.

📊

Session Progress

Mark sessions as watched or pending. See at a glance how many recordings you still need to catch up on before the next quiz.

🔒

Secure Google Login

Sign in with your Google account — no separate password to remember. Your data stays yours and is never shared with third parties.

Why LecFlow

Replace the Spreadsheet

Most BS students track lectures in a Google Sheet or a sticky-notes folder. LecFlow is what that spreadsheet was trying to be.

❌ No idea how many recordings you still haven't watched before an exam
